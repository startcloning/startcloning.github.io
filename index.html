Timeline

StartCloning

  • 1885

    First Demonstration of Artificial Embryo Twinning

    Scientist, Hans Adolf Edward Dreisch, discovered that it was possible to separate cells using a sea urchin. He shook two-celled sea urchin embryos in order to separate their cells which made the individual cells grow into two separate sea urchins. This proved that there is a separate genetic make-up for the embryos in each cell which can grow into its own individual organism.

  • 1952

    First Successful Nuclear Transfer

    Scientists Robert Briggs and Thomas King made a tadpole by transferring the nucleus of a tadpole embryo into a frog egg with the nucleus removed. Not many tadpole clones survived and the ones that did grew abnormally because cloning has less success when the nuclei is transferred from a more advanced embryo Nevertheless, this experiment proved that nuclear transfer is a possible method of cloning, and that the nucleus controls cell growth and development.

  • 1996

    Dolly The Sheep Is Created

    Scientists Ian Wilnut and Keith Campbell transferred nuclei from a cultured sheep cells grown in the laboratory into sheep egg cells without a nuclei (rather than the nuclei from early embryos) to produce lambs. They cloned a lamb from an adult sheep's somatic cell by transferring its nucleus into an egg with no nucleus. They named this lamb Dolly, whom was famous for being the first embryo carried to term by a surrogate mother, this further advanced stem cell research.

  • 1997

    First Primate Cloned

    Scientists Li Meng, John Ely, Richard Stouffer, and Don Wolf created the first primate by fusing early embryos with monkey egg cells which lacked nuclei. Next, they implanted the new embryos into surrogate mothers, which produced two monkeys, Neti and Ditto. This experiment showed scientists that monkeys can be cloned which are very similar to humans therefore, it is likely we would be able to clone humans if given the chance.

  • 2007

    Primate Embryonic Stem Cells Created by SCNT

    Scientist Shoukhrat Mitalipov and his colleagues fused an adult monkey cell with an egg cell without a nucleus, its embryonic stem cells can seperate to make any kind of cell. This proved that nuclear transfer in primates was possible and started the idea for human therapeutic cloning which can treat disease, this was a huge medical advancement.

  • 2013

    Human Embryonic Stem Cells Created by SCNT

    Scientist Shoukhrat Mitalipov and his colleagues produced a human embryo. The process they used is called somatic cell nuclear transfer and can be used to make embryonic stem cells. The stem cells made were specific to the patient they derived from.

What Is Cloning?

Definition

Cloning is when you take a living organism and replicate it to make genetically identical copies. A clone is an organism with the exact same genetic make-up as the individual it derived from.


Introduction

Scientists first discovered cloning in 1885 by using a simple sea urchin. Today they can clone things as small as a cell and as big as an entire organism such as an animal.

Along with the advantages cloning has given to the human race in medical advances, it also brings large ethical controversies related to religion and how the clone’s mentality will be affected.

These ethical issues are the reason we have not cloned a human yet, it is prohibited.

How Is Cloning Done?

somatic cell nuclear transfer.

What Is It?

Somatic Cell Nuclear Transfer was the method used to produce the famous clone, Dolly the Sheep.
Somatic cells are any cells found within the body excluding sperm and egg.

1. When doing a Somatic Nuclear Transfer, scientists remove somatic cells from an animal or human.

2. Next, they remove the nucleus from the somatic cell and transfer it to an egg cell.
This step is assisted by an electrical current, this allows the membranes to fuse and become one cell.

3. Once fertilized, it develops into an embryo which is implanted into a surrogate mother.
The animal produced is an exact copy of the animal that donated the somatic cell.

Therapeutic Cloning

embryonic stem cells.


What Is It?

The process of creating a cloned embryo is done with the sole purpose of producing embryonic stem cells. These stem cells have the same DNA as the donor cell.

Through therapeutic cloning, scientists can create tissues to replace injured or diseased tissue. This also allows them to develop new treatments for diseases.

Another benefit to therapeutic cloning is the ability to prevent rejection of foreign tissue because the new tissue is compose of the patients own genetic material.

Therapeutic cloning can play a large part in helping to save the lives of humans. Often times people who need organ transplants are not able to receive one in time or they cannot find a match. Being able to clone an organ for someone who is in desperate need is one of the many reasons why therapeutic cloning is so valuable.

Therapeutic Cloning For Parkinson's Disease

Scientists have used therapeutic cloning to help people who are suffering from Parkinson’s disease. Scientists have done various experiments utilizing a similar disease found in mice.

They inserted genetically modified stem cells into the dopamine neurons, which were then transplanted into the mice. The part of the mice’s brain lacked dopamine neurons similarly to humans with Parkinson’s disease.

Following this transplant, scientists observed that the mice showed significant improvements in mobility. This advancement allowed scientists to realise the potential of therapeutic cloning in humans. Nevertheless, therapeutic cloning must come a long way before changing the way we cure human diseases.

Benefits&Controversies

why clone? stop cloning?

The Good & The Bad

Cloning has opened so many new and amazing possibilities in the field of science.
Cloning is used in medicine, the reproduction of deceased pets, livestock, drug production, disease treatment and several other possibilities.

Along with all these great benefits that cloning has to offer, there are even more controversies to discuss. Not only does cloning raise several concerns regarding human rights, there is also the religious aspect as well.

The ethical debate over cloning humans has come to a standstill due to the overall consensus that the cloning of humans is just too dangerous.


Cloning Deceased Pets

The cloning of deceased pets has become more and more popular over the past few years.
Although there is a very high price tag, pet owners around the world are ready to do whatever it takes to keep their furry friends alive.

One very important fact to note is that these cloned animals are not exactly like the donor of its genetic material. Many different factors play a role in the non-identical clones such as the environment.
